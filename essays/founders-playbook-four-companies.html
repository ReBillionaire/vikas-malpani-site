StringBuzz (2005–2007): Start Before You're Ready

h2>

At 22, I was studying software engineering at VTU Bangalore. I was nobody. No capital, no network, no credibility. Just an uncomfortable feeling that I could build something useful.

So I built StringBuzz — a social media analytics tool ahead of when social media analytics was a category. I didn't know how to raise money. I didn't know how to hire. I didn't know how to sell. I knew how to code, and I had conviction that the problem mattered. So I did it anyway.

StringBuzz never became a meaningful business. But it taught me something invaluable: the gap between perfectionism and action is paralysis. Every day you wait for clarity, for capital, for credentials, you're not getting smarter — you're getting older. The muscle you build shipping product is real. The experience of launching to users is real. The feedback loop of iteration is real. Everything else is theory.
